## Entry Strategy: Three Concurrent Tracks

Cantus launches through three parallel tracks, each generating revenue and building the capability foundation the next phase requires.

### Track 1: Advisory & Coordination (Revenue from Month 1)

**What:** Fee-based site advisory, interconnection consulting, and tenant representation for power-heavy industrial users — leveraging Dom's Newmark Powered Industries relationships and expertise.

**First clients:**
- Hyperscale operators evaluating Texas sites (relationships from Newmark pipeline)
- Industrial developers entering powered land for the first time (Hillwood, Crow, NorthPoint — relationships already warm)
- Advanced manufacturers navigating ERCOT interconnection for new facilities

**Revenue model:** Retainer ($10-25K/month) or success fee (50-150 bps on transaction value). Target: 3-5 mandates in Year 1.

**Why this works first:** Zero capital required. Immediate credibility from Newmark track record. Every engagement generates site intelligence that feeds CII and identifies co-development and principal opportunities.

**What:** Package the site intelligence and market analysis Cantus accumulates through Tracks 1 and 2 into a subscription product for developers, capital partners, and tenants.

**First product (CII Q1 ship):**

**Revenue model:** Subscription ($50-100K/year) for capital partners and co-development partners. Free for active tenant prospects (lead generation). Internal use for Cantus decision-making.

**Why this matters for GTM:** The intelligence product is the proof-of-capability artifact. It demonstrates the analytical depth that makes co-development and advisory relationships valuable. It also becomes the data room foundation for fund formation.

## First Three Deals — Deal Archetypes

### Deal 1: Advisory Mandate (Immediate)

**Archetype:** Hyperscale operator or advanced manufacturer evaluating Texas sites. Needs ERCOT interconnection analysis, BTM feasibility, and site qualification.

**Cantus role:** Fee-based advisory. Deliver site qualification memo, energy execution plan, and tenant-site pairing recommendation.

**Revenue:** $500K-$2M (retainer or success fee).

**Strategic value:** Establishes track record. Intelligence feeds CII. Relationship may convert to co-development or tenant.

**[DOM — FILL IN]:** Which current or recent Newmark client conversations could convert to a Cantus advisory mandate?

### Deal 2: Co-Development Activation (Months 3-6)

**Archetype:** Industrial merchant developer (Hillwood/Crow tier) with 500-2,000 acres of infrastructure-adjacent land in ERCOT territory. Has done basic site work but cannot independently underwrite hyperscale power delivery.

**Cantus role:** Activation partner. Deliver interconnection strategy, BTM bridge generation plan, tenant introduction, and campus master plan.

**Revenue:** $1-2M activation fee + 20-30% carry above 8% hurdle on stabilized value.

**Strategic value:** First proof of the co-development model. Creates a reference campus for future partnerships. Carry provides long-term upside without balance sheet commitment.

### Deal 3: Principal Position (Months 6-12)

**Archetype:** Tier 1 site with extraordinary attributes — decommissioning generation with 345kV interconnection, or stranded ERCOT queue position with completed studies. Current owner is non-strategic (utility, retiring IPP, distressed developer).

**Cantus role:** Principal acquisition. Fund with balance sheet capital (or pre-formation capital from seed investors). Activate through energy execution and tenant recruitment.

**Revenue:** Development margin (15-25% on total project cost). Ongoing lease revenue. Optionality for operated capacity.

**Strategic value:** Demonstrates conviction. Anchors Fund I track record. Highest economic return.

## Sales Process

### Tenant Acquisition Funnel

1. **Identification** (CII + EDC network + Newmark relationships): Build pipeline of companies with announced or planned U.S. expansion requiring 50+ MW
2. **Qualification** (Site Qualification + Tenant Match loops): Match tenant requirements to Cantus site portfolio
3. **Proposal** (Campus specification + energy execution plan): Deliver integrated offering — site, power, building spec, timeline, community/workforce package
4. **Negotiation** (Lease/BTS terms): Structure around Advanced Industrial Spec; incorporate tenant-specific requirements
5. **Close** (Lease execution or BTS agreement): Revenue recognition; construction initiation

### Capital Partner Acquisition Funnel

1. **Identification:** Infrastructure-focused LPs (pension funds, sovereign wealth, family offices, infrastructure funds)
2. **Qualification:** Alignment on thesis, check size ($25-100M), time horizon (7-10+ years)
3. **Materials:** Platform briefing, market sizing, track record from advisory + co-development
4. **Meetings:** 10-20 qualified conversations → 3-5 serious diligence processes → 1-2 anchor commitments
5. **Fund I close:** Target $250-500M, 24-month fundraise

## Competitive Entry Timing

### Why Now (2026)

1. **ERCOT Batch Zero:** July 2026 deadline creates urgency for powered land positioning. Sites that miss Batch Zero need BTM — Cantus's core capability.
2. **GPU shortage:** All on-demand compute sold out through 2026. Hyperscalers accelerating site acquisition.
3. **BTM adoption inflection:** Share of campuses using onsite power grew 22% in six months. Structural shift, not cycle.
4. **Manufacturing construction wave:** $200B+/year in factory construction; semiconductor and battery projects consuming power at unprecedented scale.
5. **Developer gap:** No existing developer integrates data center + manufacturing + energy execution. The category doesn't exist yet.

### Window Risk

The window is open but narrowing. Established data center developers (QTS, Compass, Stack) are beginning to build energy execution capability. Industrial developers (Hillwood, Crow) are exploring powered land independently. The integration advantage exists today because no one has built it yet. First-mover in the integrated category captures partner relationships and site positions that late entrants cannot replicate.
