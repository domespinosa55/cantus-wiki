## Slide 2: The Moment

**Two factory categories. One infrastructure bottleneck.**

The U.S. is simultaneously building AI factories (data centers) and manufacturing factories (semiconductors, batteries, defense). Both require the same physical inputs: reliable power at scale and industrial-grade real estate. The bottleneck is the same: not enough developers who can deliver powered sites at the speed demand requires.

**Key data points (visual: split screen — AI factory / Manufacturing factory):**
- U.S. data center capacity: 30 GW today → 90-110 GW by 2030
- $3T+ in reshoring investment announced since 2025
- ERCOT: 410 GW requested vs. 103 GW grid capacity
- Manufacturing construction spending: 1,300% increase in electronics/computing since 2020

## Slide 6: The Mixed-Tenant Economic Insight

**This is the slide that makes the thesis click for investors.**

**Visual: 500 MW campus economics diagram**

- 300 MW anchor: Hyperscale AI compute at $90/MWh blended
- 150 MW co-tenant: Advanced manufacturing at $55/MWh (globally competitive)
- 50 MW curtailable: Flexibility, peak shaving, demand response revenue

**The mechanism:** Hyperscale tenants have high power cost tolerance ($80-110/MWh). Manufacturing tenants need globally competitive rates ($45-65/MWh). Co-locating both on a single campus with shared infrastructure and coordinated energy execution produces blended economics that make American manufacturing viable — and that no single-tenant project can replicate.

**One line:** "The reindustrialization of American manufacturing depends on solving the domestic energy cost gap. Multi-tenant campus design with hyperscale anchor demand is one of the few structural mechanisms to address it at scale."

## Slide 8: Business Model — Three Operating Models

**Visual: three columns, each with revenue type and capital intensity**

### Model 1: Developer & Landlord
- Aggregate land, execute energy, build campus, lease to tenants
- Revenue: long-duration leases, capacity payments, ancillary services
- Most replicable, most financeable

### Model 2: Co-Development Partner
- Partner with landowners; Cantus contributes capability they lack
- Revenue: activation fees, carried interest, equity participation
- Scales without consuming balance sheet

### Model 3: Operated Capacity
- Edge → Compute → Prime progression
- Revenue: compute-as-a-service, enterprise AI deployment, integrated prime contracting
- Strategic endpoint; phased entry

**Key point:** All three deployed in combination. Site attributes determine model allocation.

## Slide 9: Energy Execution — The Moat

**This slide explains why energy execution is the scarcest and most valuable developer capability.**

**The timeline gap:**
- Utility interconnection: 36-48 months
- Tenant requirement: 12-18 months
- **Cantus BTM bridge generation fills the gap**

**Four components:**
1. Large-load interconnection as ongoing infrastructure (not a project task)
2. Bridge BTM generation (gas engines, aero turbines, BESS) delivering firm power in 12-18 months
3. Permanent BTM as grid asset (ancillary services, peak shaving, demand response)
4. Equipment procurement with speculative inventory compressing lead times

**Key data:** All on-demand GPU compute sold out. Blackwell booked through Sep 2026. 38% of data centers planning onsite power by 2030.

**One line:** "Cantus campuses operate as grid assets, not just grid loads."

## Slide 12: Capital Architecture

**Visual: phased timeline showing capital mix evolution**

| Phase | Activities | Capital | Objective |
|-------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Years 1-2** | Advisory, first co-dev, initial positions | Balance sheet + advisory revenue | Establish credibility |
| **Years 3-4** | First campuses, expanded co-dev, Fund I | Fund capital + project finance | Scale the platform |
| **Years 5-7** | Multiple campuses, Fund II/III, operated capacity | Fund + project finance dominant | Compound and institutionalize |

**Four capital pools:** Balance sheet → Co-dev partner capital → Fund capital → Project finance/asset-level debt

**Principle:** Each activity funded by the capital type naturally suited to its risk/return profile.
