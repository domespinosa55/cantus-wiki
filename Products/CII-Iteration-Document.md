# CII Iteration Document

**Subtitle:** First-Principles Answers to Options A, B, and C
**Product:** Cantus Integrated Intelligence (CII)
**Classification:** Internal Working Document

## Framing

Working artifact producing raw material for the CII product description. Three views of the same underlying product:

- **Option A (Data Assets):** What Cantus accumulates — the inputs
- **Option B (Loops):** How Cantus processes — the engine
- **Option C (Surfaces):** What users interact with — the outputs

Reading order: inputs → loops → outputs. The eventual product description will reorganize around loops as the strategic backbone.

## Option B: Four Prediction-and-Resolution Loops

### Loop 1: Site Qualification
**Question:** Should Cantus pursue this site, and through which operating model?

**Inputs:** Parcel Registry, Infrastructure Adjacency Index, ERCOT Queue Tracker, Substation Capacity Index, Water Rights Cadastre, Labor Shed Atlas, Political Stakeholder Map, EDC Register.

**Output:** Site qualification memo with Tier classification, operating model recommendation (developer/landlord, co-development, operated capacity), confidence interval, and unknowns requiring diligence.

**Feedback:** Actual outcomes on pursued/declined sites calibrate scoring model over project cycles.

### Loop 2: Tenant Match
**Question:** Which tenants for which sites, and what offering structure?

**Inputs:** Tenant Demand Pipeline, Hyperscale Operator Profiles, Manufacturing Recruit Tracker, Federal Incentive DB, Tenant Reference Library + qualified sites from Loop 1.

**Output:** Ranked tenant-site pairings with recommended offering structure and commercial framework.

**Feedback:** Win/loss reasons, commercial term progression, and deal economics feed back into match logic.

### Loop 3: Energy Execution
**Question:** How does Cantus deliver power to a specific tenant-site pairing within required timeline, and at what cost?

**Inputs:** ERCOT Queue Tracker, Substation Capacity Index, Equipment Lead-Time Atlas, Ancillary Services Model, Tariff Environment, OEM Registry + tenant requirements from Loop 2 + site characteristics from Loop 1.

**Output:** Energy execution plan specifying delivery path, equipment positions, timeline, and blended cost economics.

**Feedback:** Actual time-to-power, delivered cost, equipment availability, and reliability calibrate model.

### Loop 4: Community Alignment
**Question:** What community embeddedness investment does this market/project require, and what political economy outcome does it produce?

**Inputs:** EDC Register, Labor Shed Atlas, Talent Partnership Tracker, Political Stakeholder Map, Community Impact Ledger + context from Loops 1-3.

**Output:** Community alignment plan with EDC engagement cadence, partnership investments, stakeholder engagement, and community impact commitments.

**Feedback:** Political outcomes (approval timelines, permits, abatements, community sentiment) calibrate model.

### Loop Cascade
Loops cascade: Site Qualification → Tenant Match → Energy Execution → Community Alignment. In practice they run in parallel, but the dependency structure is meaningful. CII's value is running them in coordinated fashion.

## Option C: Product Surfaces

Six user categories, twenty-three surfaces:

### Surface 1: Cantus Operating Team
- **The Map Room:** Primary dashboard — priority markets, tracked sites, active tenant pipeline, energy execution state, EDC engagement, alerts
- **Loop Workbenches:** Dedicated working surface per loop
- **Data Asset Registry:** Admin inventory of every asset, state, owner, refresh cadence
- **Decision Log:** Structured record of every significant decision + outcome (enables feedback loops)

### Surface 2: Capital Partners
- Platform Briefing (quarterly)
- Project Investment Memos (per principal project)
- Capital Partner Portal (permissioned, quarterly cadence + real-time access)
- Strategic Position Briefings (periodic synthesis)

### Surface 3: Tenants
- Tenant Pipeline Briefings (tailored per prospective tenant)
- Site and Campus Specifications (detailed technical/commercial specs)
- Tenant Operations Portal (capacity utilization, equipment performance, milestones)

### Surface 4: EDCs and Community Partners
- EDC Engagement Brief (quarterly per relationship)
- Partnership Performance Reports (per active partnership)
- Community Impact Disclosures (annual + project-specific)

### Surface 5: OEM and Counterparty Partners
- Counterparty Engagement Brief
- Co-Development Project Portal
- OEM Channel Reports

### Surface 6: Public and Media
- Cantus Public Articulation
- Thought Leadership Catalog
- Recruitment Surface
- Press and Media Coordination

## Synthesis

Data assets are inputs. Loops are processing. Surfaces are outputs. The strategic backbone is the loops — they are where CII's identity lives and what compounds over time.

### The Compounding Asset

Three mechanisms, all real, all compounding on the same engine:

1. **Decision quality:** Loops accumulate outcome data, resolution logic improves
2. **Institutional knowledge:** Every project contributes to data assets and collective understanding
3. **External credibility:** Surfaces communicate capability and outcomes, strengthening strategic position

### Q1 Shipping Shape

Ship the foundation, not the whole platform:

- Cantus Parcel Registry as master site record
- One loop fully operational (Site Qualification — cleanest dependencies)
- One or two operating team surfaces (Map Room + Site Qualification Workbench)
- Data Asset Registry as meta-tool

Ship small. Ship well. The compounding asset is **integrity** — every output trusted because inputs and processing have integrity.
