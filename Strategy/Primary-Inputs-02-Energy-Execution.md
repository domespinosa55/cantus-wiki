# Primary Inputs — Component 2: Energy as a Developer Capability

**Series:** Primary Inputs Framework (2 of 3+)
**Practice:** Cantus Coordination
**Classification:** Internal Strategy Document

## Core Thesis

Most industrial developers treat power as a site characteristic. Cantus treats power as an **output that the developer manufactures**. We do not market sites that have power. We deliver power to sites that need it.

Energy execution is a coordinated set of capabilities deployed across the project lifecycle: large-load utility interconnection studies, bridge generation for compressed timelines, permanent generation for grid services participation, and equipment procurement that compresses lead times constraining every other developer.

## The Four Components of Energy Execution

### Component 1: Large-Load Utility Interconnection

Cantus operates interconnection capability as **ongoing infrastructure**, not a single project task.

- Maintain active queue positions across multiple priority sites
- Run multiple studies in parallel
- Engage with utility planners as partners, not gatekeepers
- Use queue intelligence to inform site origination decisions
- Key utilities: Oncor, AEP, CenterPoint, ERCOT-area cooperatives
- Regulatory knowledge: PGRR144 dynamic modeling, CLR designation framework, cluster study process

The strategic insight: interconnection capability is itself a primary input. The developer that maintains queue positions across multiple sites has optionality that single-site developers cannot match.

### Component 2: Bridge Behind-the-Meter Generation

Solves the timeline gap between utility interconnection (36-48 months) and tenant requirements (12-18 months).

- **Reciprocating gas engines** (Caterpillar, Wärtsilä, Cummins): dispatchable power within 12-18 months of order
- **Aeroderivative gas turbines** (GE Vernova, Mitsubishi Power): larger blocks, similar timelines
- **BESS:** reliability support + ancillary services revenue
- Bridge generation does not need to be permanent — can be redeployed to next project once grid power arrives

See (Newmark-wiki/Intelligence/Market/Onsite-Gas-Generation-Deep-Dive) for full equipment landscape analysis.

### Component 3: Permanent BTM Generation as Grid Asset

Post-bridge generation retained as permanent grid-supportive infrastructure:

- Ancillary services markets (regulation, responsive reserve, non-spinning reserve, ECRS)
- Peak shaving capability benefiting Cantus and host utility
- Demand response programs earning capacity payments
- Reliability augmentation at premium pricing

The strategic insight: Cantus campuses operate as **grid assets**, not just grid loads. A campus that participates in grid stability is a utility partner, not a burden. This changes the political economy of every project.

### Component 4: Equipment and Long-Lead Procurement

Speculative inventory positions calibrated to expected project pipeline:

- Transformers, switchgear, gas engines, aero turbines, BESS, balance-of-plant
- Current lead times: 12-36 months depending on category
- Strategic OEM relationships: Caterpillar, Wärtsilä, Cummins, Mitsubishi Power, GE Vernova, ABB, Siemens, Tesla, Fluence, Powin

The developer that holds inventory positions can deliver on timelines competitors cannot match.

## Mixed-Tenant Campus Economics

The price tolerance spectrum creates an opportunity single-tenant projects cannot capture.

**Example: 500 MW campus design**

- **300 MW anchor:** Hyperscale AI compute at $90/MWh blended (grid + BTM + ancillary)
- **150 MW co-tenant:** Advanced manufacturing at $55/MWh blended (cross-subsidy enabled by multi-tenant configuration)
- **50 MW curtailable:** Interruptible load providing flexibility, peak shaving, demand response revenue

Economic mechanisms: shared infrastructure reduces per-tenant capex, coordinated load profiles improve generation utilization, aggregated ancillary services revenue at scale, strategic curtailment monetizes flexibility.

**The reindustrialization of American manufacturing depends on solving the domestic energy cost gap.** Multi-tenant campus design with anchor hyperscale demand is one of the few structural mechanisms to address that gap at scale — and it is the mechanism Cantus is built to execute.

## Why Energy Execution Is a Primary Input, Not a Service

A service is delivered per-transaction and depreciates. A primary input **compounds**.

- OEM relationships deepen with every procurement
- Interconnection expertise sharpens with every study
- Grid services participation builds operational track record
- Equipment procurement leverage grows with order volume

The capability accumulates as a strategic asset. Tenants engage for integrated capability, not episodic services.

## Risks

- **Capital intensity:** Bridge/permanent generation + inventory requires capital. Mitigant: phased deployment, capital partner relationships.
- **Regulatory exposure:** ERCOT evolving (PGRR144, CLR, market reforms). Mitigant: deep regulatory expertise, proactive PUCT engagement.
- **Equipment supply chain:** OEM lead times extending. Mitigant: deep OEM relationships, multi-sourcing, strategic inventory.
- **Commodity exposure:** Gas pricing affects BTM economics. Mitigant: hedging, diversified generation mix.
- **Utility relationship dependency:** Oncor, AEP, CenterPoint as gatekeepers. Mitigant: grid-supportive project design, partner orientation.
