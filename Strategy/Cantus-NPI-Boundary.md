# Cantus–NPI Boundary

*How Cantus and NPI differ, why they are separate entities, and how they sit together on a deal. Written firewall-clean: this describes the relationship at a strategic level and imports no NPI internal scoring, pipeline schema, surface rules, or client material. Author of record: Dom Espinosa.*

> Draft positioning note. Cantus-side document. The firewall with Newmark / NPI is hard — see the Cantus operating manual hard rules.

---

## The core distinction: principal vs. agent

One line carries the whole relationship: **NPI sells advice and connections for a fee; Cantus owns and operates the production for principal economics.**

NPI is an **advisor** — it grew out of the traditional commercial-real-estate brokerage model and earns transaction and advisory fees for finding, qualifying, and connecting powered sites and capacity to off-takers and developers. It takes no ownership and carries no asset risk. That is agency.

Cantus is a **principal** — the sister development company for the emerging powered-industries ecosystem. It takes ownership positions, carries balance-sheet and fund capital at risk, and earns development profit, lease/take-out, infrastructure equity, fund economics, and the back-end PPA participation. That is ownership of the production.

NPI's output — a qualified site with a matched off-taker — is *upstream* of Cantus's input — a development Cantus takes down as principal and operates.

## Side-by-side

| Dimension | NPI (advisor / agent) | Cantus (developer / principal) |
|---|---|---|
| Role | Advisor, broker, originator | Developer, owner, operator |
| Earns | Transaction & advisory fees | Development profit, rent, infra equity, fund economics, PPA back-end |
| Capital | Capital-light; no asset on the books | Capital-intensive; principal risk |
| Lineage | Traditional CRE brokerage extended into powered land + large-load power | Power-sector integrator built from first principles |
| Output | A qualified site + matched off-taker | An owned, powered, operating production |
| Duty | Fiduciary to its client | Economic interest as a principal |
| Identity | A practice line inside Newmark | Power Producer → Power Production Company → Power Studio |

## Why two entities, not one

The split is deliberate, and it does three things a single firm could not:

**It manages a real conflict.** A broker owes a fiduciary duty to its client; a principal has its own economic interest in the same transaction. One firm cannot reliably be both on the same deal. Two entities let NPI stay a clean advisor while Cantus takes principal positions — which is the deeper reason the firewall is hard, beyond IP hygiene.

**It lets each earn its native economics.** Advisory economics (fees) and principal economics (ownership, annuities, carry) have different risk, capital, and return profiles, different governance, and different investor bases. Keeping them in separate vehicles lets each be capitalized and measured on its own terms.

**It creates an origination flywheel.** NPI surfaces and qualifies opportunities; Cantus develops and owns the best of them; the principal returns fund capability that compounds. Origination feeds development; development funds capability; capability sharpens origination.

## The boundary rules — policing the shared vocabulary

The two firms look almost identical on a capability map. The same words appear on both sides — coordinating power + site + tenant + capital "as one engagement," a "power industries" practice, an intelligence/scoring layer, incentives-and-policy work — and they touch overlapping operating tools. That parallelism is exactly why the boundary needs explicit rules. The governing principle:

> **Same activity, different hat, different pay.** When the work is *advice*, it is NPI and it is paid by fee. When the work is *principal* — capital, ownership, operating risk — it is Cantus and it is paid by ownership economics.

Applied to the recurring overlaps:

| Overlapping idea | NPI expression (advice, fee) | Cantus expression (principal, ownership) |
|---|---|---|
| Stack orchestration / "one engagement" | Advising a client on how to coordinate the stack | Acting as the development quarterback who owns the outcome and the back-end |
| Power-industries / demand-vector work | Advisory mandate serving the off-taker | Principal counterparty delivering the campus and keeping the economics |
| Intelligence / scoring layer | NPI's internal tooling (stays NPI-side) | Cantus CII / DealOS as Cantus's own capability |
| Incentives, capital & policy | Advisory structuring for a client | Capital Cantus deploys and carries as principal |

The rule of thumb that keeps it clean: **if there is an asset, a balance sheet, or an operating risk involved, it is Cantus; if it is counsel, connection, or a transaction fee, it is NPI.**

## How they sit together on a deal

Both can appear on the same transaction precisely because they are paid for different things:

1. **NPI originates and qualifies** — finds the powered site, qualifies the off-taker, runs the advisory work, and earns its transaction/advisory fee. (The advisory / origination seats — the lighter-touch funnel.)
2. **Cantus takes principal** — assembles or acquires the site, powers it through Cantus Prime, builds and owns the facility, brings the execution partners (battery, generation, interconnection), and keeps development profit, operating economics, and the back-end PPA participation. (The principal seats: Cantus Development, Cantus Infrastructure / Prime, Cantus Markets.)

Mapped to the execution model: NPI occupies the **advisory seats** (closest to the orchestrator-as-advisor posture), and Cantus occupies the **principal seats** and holds the accountable, ownership-bearing role across delivery. A deal where NPI advises and Cantus develops is the clean, conflict-free pattern — two firms, two economics, one development.

## What stays firewall-separated

To keep the relationship clean at the deliverable layer:

- **No NPI client material** in Cantus documents, and none the other way.
- **No NPI internal scoring, assessment language, pipeline schema, or surface rules** imported into Cantus deliverables. Cantus uses its own framework (the v2 four-vertical architecture, CII/DealOS) in Cantus material.
- **Conflicts disclosed and managed** where NPI advises a client and Cantus is, or could become, a principal in the same opportunity.
- **Distinct economics, distinct vehicles** — fees to NPI, ownership and principal returns to Cantus; never blended in a single instrument without explicit structuring.

## One-line summary

**NPI is the advisor that originates and structures the opportunity out of the commercial-real-estate tradition; Cantus is the sister development company that takes it down as principal and owns the powered-industries production. Advice for a fee, on one side of the wall; ownership of the production, on the other.**
