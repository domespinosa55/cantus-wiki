# Which Seat Is Cantus? — Execution Operating Model, v0.1

*Companion to `Cantus-Execution-Operating-Model-v0.1.xlsx`. Working memo, not a capital-partner deliverable. Author of record: Dom Espinosa. 2026-06-05.*

---

## The artifact we started from

The Project Galaxy RACI encodes a manufacturing campus delivered as a **single accountable JV**: a builder leading vertical/horizontal construction, a "Power & Commercial" advisory seat accountable for power strategy, interconnection, power-infrastructure delivery, and O&M, and a shared **PMO + Procurement spine** that welds the seams and pre-buys long-lead iron. Twelve phases, site identification to operational, one accountable owner per phase.

Generalizing it surfaces one structural fact: **that "Power & Commercial" seat is the Cantus seat.** It is the role the v2 framework already describes — power-first, integrator/orchestrator, demand-side-outward. The example happened to fill it with an outside advisor; the question for Cantus is how much of the matrix the firm should hold itself.

(Firewall note: the generalized model strips the named partners from the example. It uses Cantus seat language only — no Newmark/NPI client material carries into Cantus working files.)

## The translation that matters

The example used **one** column for "Power & Commercial." Cantus's v2 architecture splits that across two verticals:

- **Power** → Cantus Prime (Infrastructure): power strategy, interconnection, BTM-first delivery, O&M.
- **Commercial** → Cantus Markets: capital, PPA/offtake structuring, expansion economics.

So a recommendation falls straight out of the generalization: **internally these are two verticals; externally they should present as one face to the JV.** "Cantus Prime + Cantus Markets, acting as one seat" is the cleanest way to occupy the role the example drew — Cantus carries the power-and-commercial accountability as a unit, even though the work is staffed across two verticals plus the PMO spine.

## Three postures (detailed in the "Cantus Seat Options" tab)

The open question — how much of the matrix Cantus owns — resolves into three coherent postures, each mapping to a stage of the Power Producer → Power Production Company → Power Studio arc.

**Posture A — Orchestrator / advisory (Power Producer, now).** Cantus is accountable only where its integration IP is irreplaceable: site origination, power strategy, power-infra advisory, energy management, and expansion. Build, land, design, and capital sit with partners. ~5 of 12 phases. Economics are activation fees, retainers, and carry above hurdle; balance-sheet light. This is what Cantus *is* today and what it is on any deal where it is hired in rather than leading.

**Posture B — Power & Commercial Prime (Production Company, Activation).** Cantus owns the power-and-commercial spine *and* the PMO + Procurement spine — site, power strategy, pre-con/long-lead procurement, power-infra delivery, commissioning, O&M, and expansion — while delegating land entitlement, build, and design to partners. ~7 of 12 phases. Economics add integration fees, BTM/asset equity, and PPA/commercial structuring on top of advisory. Medium capital, selective principal positions. **This is the natural through-line seat** — it is the example's "Power & Commercial + PMO" structure, Cantus-branded.

**Posture C — Integrated Developer-Principal (Power Studio, Compounding+).** Cantus additionally owns Development — entitlement, horizontal, vertical/shell, design — taking it to ~10 of 12 phases (only the tenant's own process remains external). This is the "full coverage" view in the Execution RACI tab. Economics add development profit, facility rent/take-out, and fund economics. Heavy capital — Fund I and beyond.

## The recommendation framing

The honest answer is that the seat is **phase-dependent and deal-dependent, not a single choice:**

1. **Default to Posture B as the firm's standing identity.** It is the seat the v2 framework points at, it monetizes integration without requiring the firm to win every dollar of development capital, and it is the same shape as the example that prompted this. The Power-and-Commercial-as-one-face packaging is the concrete move here.

2. **Hold Posture A as the deal-type fallback.** On any campus where a Tier-1 MPC partner or a hyperscaler is leading, Cantus takes the advisory seat without resentment — it is still accountable for the two phases (power strategy, interconnection) that gate everything else, and it keeps optionality on Posture B/C economics through carry and structuring rights.

3. **Migrate to Posture C only on Cantus-led campuses as Fund I capital lands.** Owning Development is the capital-heavy bet; it should track the Activation→Compounding capital build, not get committed to in the Building phase. The Execution RACI tab is the picture of where this ends up, not where it starts.

The single most useful framing for capital partners: **Cantus's accountability density climbs the identity arc.** Same 12-phase model, same seats — the firm simply takes the pen on more of them as it proves the integration and the capital arrives.

## Open questions this surfaces

- **Does the PMO + Procurement spine sit inside Cantus or inside the JV?** In the example it was a shared seat. Holding it is what distinguishes Posture B from Posture A — it is where long-lead procurement leverage and schedule control live. Recommend Cantus holds it from Activation on.
- **Where does Cantus Technology take a seat vs. stay informed?** Only the Compute vector pulls Technology into an accountable fit-out role (Cantus Compute as selective operator). For the other five vectors it is consulted. Worth deciding whether that is permanent or a Compounding-phase expansion.
- **Phase 9 accountability by tenant type.** The tenant owns its process — except where Cantus Compute operates the load. This is the one cell in the matrix that genuinely flips with the business model, not just the tenant.
