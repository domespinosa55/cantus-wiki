# The Advanced Industrial Spec

**Subtitle:** A Base Building Standard for the Next Tenant Class
**Author:** Dom Espinosa, Senior Managing Director — Powered Industries
**Date:** February 2026
**Reference Project:** Taylor Lot B, 1069 FM 3349, Taylor, TX (E.E. Reed / Method Architecture)

## Executive Summary

Spec industrial development is bifurcating. The bulk distribution standard that defined Class A product through the 2010s no longer matches the tenant pool driving the highest-conviction absorption: AI infrastructure operators, advanced manufacturers, EV/battery platforms, and integrated logistics users with embedded automation.

These tenants impose a consistent retrofit pattern on traditional buildings — heavier power, denser MEPF, structural augmentation, upgraded slab, expanded office, tenant-specific process integration. Each retrofit costs 2-4x its base-build equivalent and consumes 6-18 months of occupancy delay.

The Advanced Industrial Spec pre-positions these features in the base building.

## The Reverse-Engineering Premise

Reference: Taylor Lot B — a 2025 delivery in Taylor, TX with Tesla as apparent tenant. Building delivered as bulk distribution spec but undergoing extensive upgrades:

- Two-story curtain wall office entry with substantial millwork
- Dense overhead MEPF with fire sprinkler/pump/HVAC complexity beyond distribution norms
- SDT and tenant-specific rack systems integrated during base building
- Switchgear delivery and energization as critical milestone (service capacity above standard)
- Slab joint filler/sealer treated as critical-path finish

If the tenant pool is consistently asking for the same upgrades, the developer who pre-builds them captures absorption, rent premium, and cap rate compression.

## Spec Comparison by Building System

| System | Traditional Spec | Advanced Industrial Spec | Base Build Premium | Retrofit Cost |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Power | 2,000-3,000A at 480V; single transformer pad | 4,000-6,000A minimum; dual conduit runs; switchgear room sized 2x; gen pad pre-positioned | $15-25/SF | $40-80/SF + 9-18mo queue |
| Structural | 20-25 PSF roof live load; code-minimum columns | 35-40 PSF roof live load; mezzanine-ready columns/footings (125 PSF); pre-engineered tilt-wall knockouts | $3-6/SF | Often impossible (4-8mo offline) |
| Slab | 6" standard PSI; late-phase sealer | 8-10" higher PSI; FF50/FL35 flatness (AGV/AMR compatible); joint filler at pour; pre-cored utility penetrations | $2-4/SF | $8-15/SF + 6-10wks offline |
| MEPF | Code-minimum sprinkler; standard fire pump | ESFR for high-hazard Group A (lithium-ion, EV, high-pile data); oversized fire pump/water; HVAC mains stubbed; BMS conduit pre-installed | $8-12/SF | $20-35/SF + occupancy delays |
| Office Shell | Single-story spec office (3-5% GLA); standard storefront | Two-story curtain wall entry; elevated office slab with under-floor access; restroom cores and elevator shaft pre-built | $10-15/SF (office) | 2-3x cost + 4-6mo in occupied building |
| Site / Civil | Standard truck court; code-minimum trailer parking | Reinforced approach slabs all docks; 1.5x trailer parking; secondary utility easements; detention sized for future impervious cover | $3-5/SF blended | Re-permitting: 6-18mo in TX |

## Economic Argument

| Metric | Traditional Spec | Advanced Industrial Spec |
|--------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Base build premium | — | $40-70/SF |
| Equivalent retrofit cost (post-CO) | — | $80-150/SF |
| Tenant rent loss during retrofit | — | 6-18 months |
| Cap rate spread | Baseline | 50-100 bps tighter |
| Lease-up velocity premium | Market | 6-12 months faster |
| Illustrative value creation (500,000 SF) | — | $40-60M (vs. $20M premium) |

On a 500,000 SF building: $40/SF premium = ~$20M additional cost. Against that, $2/SF rent premium at 75bps cap rate compression generates $40-60M of value creation. The spec premium pays for itself before lease-up completes.

## Strategic Positioning

The Advanced Industrial Spec is the operating expression of the Powered Land thesis. The differentiator is not substation proximity alone — queue position, MW availability, and interconnection economics are necessary but not sufficient. Developers who win the AI/advanced manufacturing tenant pool deliver building product that matches where the tenant is actually going.

Integrates all three Cantus practice lines:

- **Powered Land:** Site selection, substation mapping, interconnection structuring alongside building program
- **Tenant Advisory:** Pre-leasing and BTS structuring against defined product spec, TI economics modeled against base-build premiums
- **Capital Markets:** Cap rate compression and absorption velocity translated into exit value
