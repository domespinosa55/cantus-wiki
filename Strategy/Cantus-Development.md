# Cantus Development

*Operating vertical 1 of 4 in the v2 Cantus architecture. Owns Power Land and Power Facilities. Direct principal. Creates the sites.*

---

## What This Vertical Is

Cantus Development is the **site-creation vertical** of the firm. Its scope is the assembly of raw land, the entitlement and configuration of that land for industrial use, and the construction of the Power Facilities that sit on it. The deliverable is a powered, configured, ready-to-operate site that subsequent verticals (Infrastructure, Technology, Markets) build the rest of the integrated production on top of.

Cantus Development is a **direct principal** in both of its Power sub-industries — Power Land and Power Facilities. The vertical takes ownership positions (fee simple, ground lease, build-to-suit landlord, master-developer) rather than operating as advisor, intermediary, or counterparty. Together with Cantus Markets, it is one of the two direct-principal verticals; Cantus Infrastructure operates as integrator + counterparty, and Cantus Technology operates as orchestrator + selective operator.

The firm-level value-creation sentence: *Development creates the sites.*

| [[Strategy/Power-Sector/Power-Facilities]] | Direct principal — facility developer, owner, master-developer of multi-tenant Intelligent Campuses |

The two sub-industries are operated as a single vertical because the value-creation activity is structurally continuous: assembled land transitions into entitled land, into interconnection-attached land, into configured site, into built facility. The boundary between Land and Facilities is operationally fluid; the vertical that operates one operates the other.

## Operating Model

Cantus Development's core operating model is **the Intelligent Campus** — a multi-tenant, master-planned, integrated-infrastructure development that combines assembled Land + multiple Facilities + shared substation + shared cooling/water + shared fiber + shared workforce-and-community function. The Intelligent Campus is the firm's product class and the structural articulation of what makes Cantus Development distinct from a standard MPC developer or BTS facility developer.

Three operating patterns:

**Cantus-led campus.** Cantus Development assembles the Land independently, entitles it under Cantus control, develops Facilities directly or through Cantus-controlled JVs, and operates as the master-developer of the campus. Capital partners (per [[_parameters]] Capital Partner Categories) take principal positions through Fund I (Years 3–4) or co-investment vehicles. Tenants take ground lease, BTS lease, or build-to-suit ownership positions.

**Joint-venture campus.** Cantus Development partners with Tier-1 MPC developers (Hillwood, Crow Holdings, Howard Hughes, Majestic Realty per [[_parameters]]) under JV structures. The MPC partner brings master-planning capability, capital, and political relationships; Cantus brings Power-sector integration intelligence and demand-side orchestration. JV structures specify carry, control, and brand allocation.

**Brownfield reposition.** Cantus Development acquires retiring industrial sites (coal plants, steel mills, refineries) with transferable interconnection rights and repositions them for new Power-sector use. The information advantage on which brownfields carry interconnection-rights value is one of Cantus Development's structural differentiators.

## Deliverables

Cantus Development produces five layered deliverables:

1. **Raw land control** — deeded ownership, option, ground lease, ROFR on candidate sites. The first product.
2. **Entitled land** — zoning, environmental, water rights, transmission access, interconnection queue position confirmed. Value creation visible in the price gradient from raw to entitled ($2,500–$10,000/acre to $25,000–$75,000/acre in Texas reference per [[_parameters]]).
3. **Interconnection-attached land** — sites with queued, studied, or executed interconnection positions. Premium pricing reflects the gating-constraint role interconnection plays.
4. **Configured site** — graded, prepared, with utilities to fence, civil work complete. Ready for vertical construction. Price reference ~$800K/acre and above per [[_parameters]] (Texas powered land premium).
5. **Built Facility on the site** — data hall shell + critical environment, fab building + cleanroom, gigafactory shell + dry room, defense facility + secure perimeter, biopharma cGMP suite. The Facility deliverable, with operating systems handed off to the tenant under lease, BTS, or owned-occupier structures.

The Intelligent Campus aggregates these deliverables across a multi-tenant master plan, with shared substation, fiber, water, security, and workforce-and-community function as the integration layer.

## Sub-Vertical Activities

Cantus Development's operational scope contains four primary activity classes:

**Land assembly and entitlement.** The ground-game function — identifying landowner counterparties, structuring assembly across multi-parcel candidate sites, navigating Texas-specific surface-mineral-water rights complexity, negotiating with adjacent landowners over easements, surveying, environmental review, zoning, and infrastructure cost-sharing with local government. The Texas reference jurisdiction is the working assumption; expansion to Virginia, Arizona, Georgia, and select Midwest corridors is the modeled extension over the strategic horizon.

**Interconnection-rights aggregation.** The Power Land sub-industry's gating constraint is interconnection access. Cantus Development specializes in acquiring sites with executed or queue-advanced interconnection, in repositioning brownfields with transferable interconnection rights, and in structuring TSP relationships that route new interconnection studies through the firm. CenterPoint and ERCOT relationships per [[_parameters]] are central.

**Facility development and master-planning.** Once Land is assembled and entitled, Cantus Development designs, finances, and constructs the Facilities that sit on it. The vertical operates with specialized mission-critical consultants (electrical, mechanical, civil, cleanroom, dry room, cGMP, defense-clearance), with primary EPCs (Mortenson, Holder, DPR, Whiting-Turner, Kiewit, Burns & McDonnell, Black & Veatch), and with the BTM equipment supply chain coordinated through Cantus Infrastructure.

**Multi-tenant orchestration.** A single Cantus Intelligent Campus often hosts multiple Power Industries operators (a hyperscale AI tenant + an advanced manufacturing tenant + a critical minerals tenant on shared infrastructure). The orchestration across tenant covenants, lease structures, infrastructure cost-sharing, and operational interfaces is a Cantus Development capability that standalone MPC developers do not natively have.

## Capital Architecture

Cantus Development's capital architecture is the most capital-intensive of the four verticals. The vertical absorbs the bulk of the firm's principal capital deployment, primarily through:

- **Balance-sheet positions on initial Cantus Land assembly.** Firm-formation phase (current) operates on Dom and founding partner balance sheet.
- **Fund I principal positions** (Years 3–4). Target $250M–$500M per [[_parameters]]. Primarily deployed to Land assembly + Facility construction across Cantus-led campuses.
- **Co-investment vehicles.** Single-deal and multi-deal vehicles for capital partners taking principal positions alongside Fund I.
- **Subsequent multi-fund family** (Years 5–7). Modeled at $1.5–3B committed by Year 6 per [[_parameters]].
- **REIT and infrastructure-fund take-out** (Years 5+). Stabilized Cantus Facilities (especially long-tenor BTS leases to IG hyperscale tenants) are candidates for sale-leaseback or REIT take-out at cap-rate compression.

The Land and Facility capital intensity per [[Strategy/Power-Sector/Power-Land]] and [[Strategy/Power-Sector/Power-Facilities]]: $2,500–$10,000/acre raw, $25,000–$75,000/acre entitled, ~$800,000/acre powered, then $10–40M per MW for built data center Facility (the largest Facility class for AI demand vector). A single 500MW Cantus Intelligent Campus at full build can carry $5–15B+ of facility capex alone, plus Land assembly, plus Infrastructure capex, plus tenant Machine deployment.

## What Cantus Development Is Not

Not a residential MPC developer. Not a logistics REIT. Not a pure data center REIT competing with Equinix or Digital Realty for individual cabinet sales. Not a hyperscale operator. Not a fab operator. Not a defense prime. Cantus Development's specific posture is **multi-tenant powered-industrial assembly with integrated demand-side intelligence**, expressed through the Intelligent Campus product class — structurally adjacent to but distinct from each of the above.

## Open Questions

To resolve as the framework matures:

- **Permian-specific strategy.** The Permian multi-use overlap (surface + mineral + water + power) creates economics not available elsewhere. Whether Cantus Development operates in the Permian directly, partners into it (TPL Corporation, LandBridge are the platform comparables), or stays out is an explicit strategic call carried in [[Strategy/Power-Sector/Power-Land]].
- **Water-rights treatment.** AI cooling demand has elevated water rights to a binding constraint at scale. Whether water-rights underwriting sits inside Cantus Development as a sub-activity or as a parallel Foundation-like capability needs explicit treatment.
- **Multi-class Facility integration on a single campus.** Standard product or special case? The framework currently treats multi-class as a Cantus differentiator; the operating playbook should specify.
- **Brand strategy for Cantus Intelligent Campus.** Master-branded (every Cantus campus carries the Cantus name) or sub-branded (each campus has its own brand under a Cantus parent).
- **JV vs. Cantus-led split criteria.** As Tier-1 MPC partners build competing powered-industrial platforms, the JV terms (carry, control, brand) need calibration. Standard pattern vs. case-by-case to be specified.
- **Geographic extension sequence.** ERCOT primary; PJM and Virginia next; Arizona, Georgia, select Midwest after. The sequencing criteria and the timing should be explicit in the business plan rewrite.
