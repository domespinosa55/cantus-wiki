# Primary Inputs — Component 1: Infrastructure-Dense Land Aggregation

**Series:** Primary Inputs Framework (1 of 3+)
**Practice:** Cantus Coordination
**Classification:** Internal Strategy Document

## Core Thesis

The binding constraint on hyperscale and AI infrastructure deployment is not real estate — it is the bundle of infrastructure attributes already attached to specific parcels. Grid interconnection queue position, substation adjacency, transmission easements, gas pipeline rights-of-way, rail access, and water rights are physically immobile, slow to replicate, and appreciating faster than the underlying land.

Cantus's origination thesis is a search for **embedded interconnection optionality** — sites where someone else has already done the hardest, slowest, and most expensive work of grid attachment, and where the current owner is non-strategic with respect to the highest-and-best AI infrastructure use case.

## Origination Funnel: Three-Tier Structure

Organized by **attribute being acquired**, not category of seller.

### Tier 1 — Active Interconnection Infrastructure
Highest value, hardest to source. Replacement-cost infrastructure that cannot be recreated within hyperscale deployment timelines.

- Decommissioning conventional generation (coal, legacy gas) with 345kV interconnection, switchyards, cooling water rights
- Wind/solar projects beyond useful life with existing interconnection agreements
- Stranded ERCOT queue positions (studies completed, project economics failing)
- Active substations with available capacity (138kV+)

### Tier 2 — Latent Infrastructure Adjacency
Medium value, more available. Partial infrastructure activatable through Cantus capability.

- Industrial merchant developers with substation work done but unprogrammed acreage (Hillwood, Crow Holdings, Majestic, NorthPoint, Stonepeak portfolio)
- Refinery/petrochemical adjacent land (Houston Ship Channel, Beaumont-Port Arthur)
- Rail-served brownfields with capital structure failures
- Co-op and municipal utility territories with available capacity not in standard ERCOT queue analysis (Pedernales, Bluebonnet, Bryan, Garland, Denton)

### Tier 3 — Raw Land with Strategic Positioning
Lower value at acquisition, higher availability. Requires activation work.

- Stranded MPC industrial parcels
- Filtered agricultural land near transmission corridors (~5% of universe qualifies)
- E&P surface land (ConocoPhillips, Pioneer, Devon)
- Filtered timber land along ERCOT corridors (East Texas, I-20)
- Barge access sites (Mississippi, Gulf Coast, Ohio River — broader MISO/PJM relevance)
- BRAC and federal land (long lead times, extraordinary infrastructure, DoD AI interest)

## Co-Development as Scaling Mechanism

For MPC and industrial merchant developer categories, Cantus does not pursue land acquisition. These owners are positioned as **partners, not counterparties**.

The structural insight: most MPC and industrial developers control significant land but lack the technical capability to convert holdings into hyperscale-ready powered industrial assets. They cannot independently underwrite ERCOT interconnection, OEM relationships, BTM gas economics, hyperscale offtake structures, or substation engineering.

### Deal Structures

| Structure | Mechanics | Best Fit |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Activation fee + promote | Fixed fee + back-end promote on sale/stabilization | Early proof-of-concept; uncertain partners |
| Co-development with carried interest | 20-30% carry above hurdle; partner contributes land, Cantus contributes capability | Sophisticated institutional partners (Hines/Trammell Crow model) |
| Land lease + revenue share | Below-market ground rent for revenue share on tenant leases | Partners committed to permanent ownership |
| Pre-negotiated pad sales | Forward pricing post-activation; Cantus earns dev fees + activation spread | Family-office and smaller developers |

### Priority Co-Development Partners

- **Tier 1 (most aligned):** Hillwood (Perot family), Crow Holdings, Howard Hughes Holdings (Pershing Square private), Majestic Realty
- **Tier 2 (worth pursuing):** Newland Communities, Stratford Land, select Hines mandates, Republic Property Group
- **Tier 3 (selective):** Regional industrial merchants and family-office landowners

## Cantus Capability Layer (The Moat)

The integrated stack that makes Cantus a credible partner:

- ERCOT interconnection expertise and queue position management
- OEM relationships: Caterpillar, Baker Hughes, Doosan, Kawasaki
- Behind-the-meter gas economics and tariff arbitrage modeling
- Substation engineering, load study coordination, utility interface
- Hyperscale and BTM tenant relationships
- Hopkins-class deal intelligence: site identification, queue analysis, opportunity scoring

No traditional landowner or merchant developer holds this capability internally. No traditional CRE advisor or REIT operator integrates at this depth.

## Sequencing

**Years 1-2:** Advisory and selective co-development. 3-5 Tier 1/2 partnerships under activation-fee or pad-sale structures. Newmark relationships and Hopkins intelligence as origination engine.

**Years 3-4:** Co-development at scale + first balance sheet deals. 5-10 activated sites. Fund I ($250M-$500M) for principal positions on highest-conviction sites.

**Years 5+:** Integrated platform. Co-development as permanent pillar; Fund II/III for direct acquisitions. Structural similarity to Hines/Brookfield with fee streams, balance sheet, and co-development engine.

## Strategic Risks

- **Partner adverse selection:** Sophisticated landowners may internalize Cantus capability. Mitigant: continuous movement up value chain into compute hosting and capacity sales.
- **Information leakage:** Each partnership exposes the playbook. Mitigant: intelligence layer (Hopkins, OEM density) is not replicable even after observing process.
- **Capital efficiency ceiling:** Asset-light models cap enterprise value. Mitigant: Year 3+ balance sheet vehicle for conviction acquisitions.
- **Promote compression:** Hurdle-and-carry weakens as more capital enters. Mitigant: capture Tier 1 attributes where pricing power persists.

Our capability is the technical, regulatory, and commercial infrastructure required to interconnect, energize, and tenant powered land at scale."
