
Framework RewriteL

POWER SECTOR: **Sub-industries**
1. Power Assets — owned, long-tenor physical infrastructure that produces and delivers electrons (generation, transmission, distribution, substations, storage, interconnection)
2. Power Equipment — industrial manufacturing of hardware for Power Assets (turbines, transformers, switchgear, inverters, storage systems)
3. Power Services — engineering, procurement, construction, operations, and maintenance for Power Assets and Power Facilities (EPC firms, specialty contractors)
4. Power Land — real estate optimized for power-intensive industrial use (assembled, entitled, configured sites)
5. Power Industries — demand-side industrial operators consuming power at scale to produce commodities (the six demand vectors: compute, supply chain manufacturing, energy transition manufacturing, advanced manufacturing, defense industrial base, biomanufacturing and critical materials)
6. Power Commodities — the high-value commodities produced by Power Industries (compute capacity, battery cells, semiconductor wafers, refined critical minerals, etc.)
7. Power Capital — financial intermediaries capitalizing the sector (infrastructure funds, sovereign wealth, project finance, dedicated Power-sector PE)
8. Power Markets — financial and physical trading of power, capacity, ancillary services (ISOs, trading firms, structured product providers)
9. Power Retail — customer-facing distribution and resale of electricity to end users
10. Power Technology — software, analytics, grid management, demand response, the digital layer across the sector

Power Assets: 
- **Naming of the Cantus arm.** Does _Cantus Supply_ or _Cantus Infrastructure_ read better as the operating arm spanning Power Assets, Power Equipment, and Power Services? _Supply_ is sharper and reads as the integrator across the three supply-side sub-industries. _Infrastructure_ carries more institutional weight and aligns with how capital partners taxonomize the asset class. Recommend deferring the call until Power Equipment and Power Services are drafted, since the name has to fit all three.
- **Where does BTM generation sit at the Power Assets / Power Industries boundary?** A 500MW BTM gas plant inside a hyperscale fence, owned by the data center operator, is structurally a Power Asset built into a Power Industries entity. The framework needs a defined treatment that lets capital partners and counterparties see the asset on its own terms rather than buried inside an industrial operating P&L.
- **At what threshold does Cantus become a direct Power Asset operator?** The current framework defers this to Years 5–7. The threshold logic — what specifically would trigger Cantus to own and operate generation, transmission, or storage rather than orchestrate it — should be articulated explicitly in the business plan rewrite. Candidate trigger: integrated microgrid control inseparable from the demand-side operating envelope.
- **How is regulated transmission treated when Cantus is not an owner?** Cantus's economic interest in transmission is structural (load served, interconnection rights) rather than equity. The framework should account for that asymmetry rather than treat transmission as a parallel asset class to generation in the Cantus production stack.
