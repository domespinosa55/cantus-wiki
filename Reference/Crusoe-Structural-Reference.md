# Crusoe — Structural Reference

> **Internal use only. Not for external Cantus articulation.** Crusoe is a structural comparable that informs how operator-developers package modular compute against stranded power. It is not an identity claim, not a positioning frame, and not an external citation. The Cantus voice does not invoke Crusoe in capital-partner, tenant, or partner-facing material. This page exists to preserve the structural lessons; nothing on this page should appear verbatim in external output.

---

## What Crusoe Is

GPU cloud operator originally built on flare-gas capture — colocating modular data centers with stranded natural gas at oil-well sites and converting waste gas into AI compute revenue. Pivoting toward larger-scale AI compute infrastructure.

## Why It Sits in the Internal Reference

Three structural lessons:

1. **Vertical integration of energy and compute changes the unit economics.** Crusoe owns the power source, the modular compute, and the customer relationship. The friction tax that single-tier operators absorb (negotiating across separate land, power, and tenant counterparties) is internalized.

2. **Modular packaging compresses time-to-operational.** Where conventional campus development is bound by transmission interconnection cadence, modular gas-to-compute deployments can energize on the order of months once a site is identified.

3. **The model only scales so far.** Stranded-gas economics work for distributed modular footprints. They do not directly translate to campus-scale loads where grid interconnection, multi-tenant flexibility, and capital architecture dominate the equation. Above a certain MW threshold, the integrated developer's problem becomes the four-layer problem Cantus addresses.

## What This Reference Does Not Do

- It does not position Cantus as "the BlackRock of X" or "the Crusoe of Y." Cantus constructs its own category. Comparable-driven identity claims are a hard ban.
