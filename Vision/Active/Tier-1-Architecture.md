# Tier 1 Architecture

*Open question. The new Cantus cowork context introduced three structural moves that don't yet fully reconcile with the existing Framework documents. Tier 1 is the seam where those moves land — and that seam needs to hold under capital partner scrutiny. Working through this carefully before editing the downstream documents.*

---

## What Triggered This

The new operating context (May 2026) introduced:

1. **Cantus Operations / Cantus Infrastructure split** — Operations = Tiers 1-2; Infrastructure = Tiers 3-4. Net-new firm-level organizing structure.
2. **Cantus Edge moved into the Technology Integration foundation** — explicitly *not* standalone Tier 1 anymore.
3. **Cantus Prime as "customer-agnostic infrastructure prime contractor archetype"** — replaces Forge. The word "archetype" suggests a strategic posture, not a discrete business unit.

The existing Framework Part 2 still defines Tier 1 as "Operated Capacity" with the Edge → Compute → Forge progression. The Forge → Prime substring was patched, but the underlying *Tier 1 = Operated Capacity (Edge → Compute → Prime)* framing now sits in tension with the cowork context's repositioning of Edge and re-characterization of Prime.

This document exists to think through what Tier 1 actually is in the new model, before mechanically updating the Framework docs to match.

---

## What Has Been Decided (Locked)

These are not open. Don't relitigate without explicit reason.

- **Cantus Forge is retired.** Cantus Prime replaces it conceptually, but the substitution is not 1:1.
- **Cantus Operations = Tiers 1-2; Cantus Infrastructure = Tiers 3-4.** This is a firm-level organizing decision, not a tier definition change.
- **Cantus Edge sits within the Technology Integration foundation** in the long-term trajectory framing. It is not a standalone Tier 1 unit.
- **Two Foundations are Labor/Community and Technology Integration.** They sit beneath the four-tier stack, not within it.

## What Is Open

### Question 1 — Is "Operated Capacity" still Tier 1?

The Framework Part 2 names Tier 1 as Operated Capacity. The cowork context describes Cantus Operations as Tiers 1-2. So Tier 1 is *part of* Cantus Operations, but is it still specifically "Operated Capacity"? Or has Tier 1 shifted to mean something closer to "Tenant-facing operations and integrated prime contracting"?

**Sub-questions:**
- Does "Operated Capacity" survive as the Tier 1 label, or does it get renamed?
- If renamed, what's the new label? Candidates: Integrated Operations, Tenant Platform Operations, Operated Capacity & Integration, Cantus Operating Capability.
- If the label survives, what does it now contain — given Edge has moved out and Prime is an archetype?

### Question 2 — What does Tier 1 actually contain in the new model?

If we accept that Edge has moved into the Technology Integration foundation and Prime is an archetype rather than a unit, Tier 1's contents are uncertain. Candidates:

- **(a)** Cantus Compute as the lone operating business + tenant-facing campus operations + the operating expression of CII for tenants
- **(b)** The integrated prime contractor function (the operating expression of the Prime archetype) at the customer interface — i.e., Prime *is* Tier 1
- **(c)** Tenant-facing platform operations only (campus management, capacity allocation, tenant services); compute hosting is Tier 2; Prime is cross-tier
- **(d)** Tier 1 is "Operated Outcomes" — a category that contains whatever the firm is actively delivering to a customer (compute capacity, integrated prime delivery, edge AI deployments handled via the Technology Integration foundation), with the specific contents varying by customer

Each implies different capital intensity, capability requirements, and capital-partner narrative.

### Question 3 — Where does Cantus Prime live structurally?

The cowork context calls Prime "the customer-agnostic infrastructure prime contractor archetype." Three readings:

- **As a Layer 4 strategic position** — Prime is what Cantus *becomes* as a firm. Cuts across all four tiers. The Tier 1/2/3/4 stack itself is the Prime delivery mechanism.
- **As a Tier 1 business unit** — Prime is the operating arm at the customer interface. Tier 1 = Cantus Prime.
- **As a Tier 2 capability** — Prime is a way of organizing Tenant Platform delivery — integrated prime contracting at the campus level.

The "archetype" language reads more like the first option (Layer 4 strategic position) than a discrete tier unit. But the existing Framework places Forge (the predecessor concept) at Tier 1 as the "strategic endpoint" of Operated Capacity. If Prime inherits Forge's tier placement, it's Tier 1. If Prime is genuinely an archetype, it doesn't sit in any single tier.

### Question 4 — What does Cantus Compute become?

If Edge moves into a foundation and Prime becomes an archetype, **Compute is the only "operated business" with clear tier residency left in the trajectory.** Three options:

- **(a)** Compute is Tier 1 standalone. The trajectory becomes: build Compute as the primary Tier 1 business; Edge supports it from the Technology Integration foundation; Prime emerges as the firm's strategic position once Compute matures.
- **(b)** Compute merges into Tier 2 (Tenant Platform). Cantus operates compute hosting as part of the campus offering rather than as a separate business — making Tier 2 a richer "tenant + capacity" offering.
- **(c)** Compute remains a discrete operating unit but spans Tiers 1 and 2. The compute capacity is Tier 1; the campus that hosts it is Tier 2.

### Question 5 — Capital partner narrative implications

Whatever Tier 1 becomes, the framing has to be defensible to a sophisticated LP under follow-up questions. Open considerations:

- **Capital intensity** — Tier 1 currently carries the implication of being the most capital-intensive operating commitment. If Tier 1 contents change, the capital story changes.
- **Reversibility** — the Framework currently says Operated Capacity is reversible at each step. If Tier 1 becomes something different, is reversibility still a property?
- **Earned right** — the Framework distinguishes what Cantus *is* (Tier 4 + 3 + 2 with selective Tier 1) from what Cantus *is becoming* (full Tier 1 maturity). Whatever Tier 1 becomes has to preserve this distinction.
- **Slide 6 of the Investor Deck** is the mixed-tenant economics insight. Slide 4 (or similar) is the four-tier diagram. The diagram needs to read clean to a first-time viewer.

## What Tier 1 Definitely Is *Not*

To narrow the space:

- **Not the Foundations.** Edge moved *out* of Tier 1 into Technology Integration. So Tier 1 ≠ foundations.
- **Not Tier 2.** Tier 2 is Tenant Platform — multi-tenant powered campuses, mixed economics. Tier 1 sits above it (closer to the customer / outcome layer).
- **Not Capital Architecture (Layer 3).** Tier 1 is operating; Layer 3 is financial.
- **Not Strategic Position (Layer 4).** Tier 1 is a unit of the firm; Layer 4 is what the firm becomes.
- **Not the entire firm.** Cantus Operations = Tiers 1 + 2; Cantus Infrastructure = Tiers 3 + 4. Tier 1 is one of four.

## Provisional Hypothesis (For Dom To Test)

The hypothesis below is Hopkins's best read after the analysis above. **It is a proposal, not a decision.** Mark up, reject, or refine.

> **Tier 1 is "Operated Capacity & Integrated Delivery."** It contains:
> - **Cantus Compute** as the named operating business (compute hosting and operations)
> - **The integrated delivery function** that sits at the customer interface — the operating expression of the Cantus Prime archetype, applied per customer
>
> **Cantus Prime is a Layer 4 strategic position** — what Cantus becomes as a firm — and the Tier 1 integrated delivery function is how that position gets expressed in customer-facing operations.
>
> **Cantus Edge** lives in the Technology Integration foundation and supports tenants across Tiers 1 and 2 (and indirectly Tier 3 via energy-management software). Edge is a capability the firm offers tenants, not a unit of the four-tier stack.
>
> **Long-term trajectory** within Tier 1: build integrated delivery capability through customer-by-customer engagements (Year 1-4) → Compute scales as the named operating business (Year 5-7) → Prime archetype is fully earned at Layer 4 (Year 8+).
>
> **Capital story** — Tier 1 capital intensity grows over phases. Year 1-4 light (delivery capability + selective Compute deployments). Year 5-7 medium (multi-campus Compute footprint). Year 8+ heavy (operating presence across major customers, earning the Prime archetype).

If this hypothesis lands, the downstream changes are:
- Framework Part 2: Tier 1 section rewritten — Operated Capacity expands to "Operated Capacity & Integrated Delivery"; Edge removed from Tier 1; Prime moved to Layer 4 articulation; Compute becomes the named Tier 1 business
- Executive Summary: vertical-integration bullet for Tier 1 updated
- Investor Deck Outline: Tier 1 row in the four-tier table updated; Slide on Operated Capacity progression updated
- Framework Part 1: short cross-reference adjustment if any
- Cowork-context terminology table: clarify that Prime is Layer 4 archetype, not Tier 1 unit
