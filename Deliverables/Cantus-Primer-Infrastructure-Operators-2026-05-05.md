For several years I've been working at the intersection of AI infrastructure, advanced manufacturing reshoring, and the energy that powers both. What I keep seeing is one problem expressed in different vocabularies, and a structural gap nobody is built to fill. This is what Cantus is for.

## The backdrop

Three forces have converged. AI compute is driving facility power densities to 200–400+ watts per square foot — roughly forty times traditional commercial — with single training clusters consuming more electricity than small cities. Advanced manufacturing is reshoring under CHIPS and IRA, with semiconductor fabs and battery cell plants requiring 100–200+ MW of continuous power at tolerances the grid alone cannot guarantee. America's eighteen billion square feet of industrial base, much of it more than forty years old, was built for a different power profile entirely. The gap is producing demand at a pace the existing development industry was never built to serve.

## Why nobody else fits

Each industry adjacency sees one face of the problem. Real estate firms find sites and structure transactions but cannot evaluate interconnection feasibility or load-profile economics. Energy firms understand power markets and generation but lack the customer relationships and real estate transaction structures the demand side needs. Technology and capital firms understand the demand but cannot evaluate site power feasibility or structure capital across tax credits, behind-the-meter generation, and institutional equity. Engineering consultants do the technical analysis but cannot capture the full value chain. The integrated capability barely exists today. The bottleneck for this cycle is execution capacity. Not real estate. Not capital.

> *Time-to-Operational is the deliverable that matters, and nobody is reliably providing it.*

## What Cantus does

Cantus is the integrated developer for power-heavy industrial demand — the firm I'm building to fill the gap above. It operates across all four tiers under one capability base, in two umbrellas: **Cantus Infrastructure** (Tier 4 land aggregation with embedded interconnection optionality, Tier 3 energy execution) and **Cantus Operations** (Tier 2 multi-tenant Intelligent Campuses, Tier 1 operated capacity). Two foundations cut across the stack: Labor and Community embeddedness, and Technology Integration native to the customer category. CII is the operating system. In practice the integrated capability shows up as large-load utility interconnection at hundreds of MW; **Bridge-to-Grid** generation (reciprocating engines, aeroderivative turbines, BESS) delivering firm power in 12–18 months while interconnection completes on the 36–48 month track; multi-tenant campus economics structured 300 anchor + 150 co-tenant + 50 curtailable to produce hyperscale-grade reliability at $90/MWh blended for AI and $55/MWh for manufacturing — globally competitive without subsidy. Operated capacity then comes in three stages: Cantus Edge (on-prem AI for tenants), Cantus Compute (distributed GPU hosting), and **Cantus Prime** — the customer-agnostic infrastructure prime contractor archetype, and the strategic endpoint.

## Where this ends

The unified customer category Cantus serves is industrial firms running input-transformation arbitrage at globally competitive scale. AI Factory and Manufacturing Factory are the dominant current expressions; the category extends as the firm matures. Strategic endpoint: **Cantus Prime**, the integrated prime across land, energy, labor, technology, and capacity. Multi-decade construction. Modeled firm horizon: $100M Year 6 FCF, $300–500M by Year 10, $750M–$1B by Year 15.
